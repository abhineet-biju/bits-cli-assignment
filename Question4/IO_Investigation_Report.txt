Technical Explanation:
Linux treats many resources as files, including terminals, regular files, and device interfaces. Processes access these resources through file descriptors. Standard input, standard output, and standard error are represented by descriptors 0, 1, and 2. Redirection changes where these descriptors send or receive data.
